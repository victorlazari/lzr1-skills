## 1. Strategic Planning

### Strategy Framework

| Component | Description | Output |
|---|---|---|
| Vision | Where are we going? (10+ years) | Vision statement |
| Mission | Why do we exist? | Mission statement |
| Strategy | How will we win? | Strategic pillars |
| Objectives | What will we achieve? (1-3 years) | OKRs |
| Tactics | What will we do? (quarterly) | Initiatives |
| Metrics | How do we measure? | KPIs |

### Strategic Planning Process

| Phase | Activities | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| Assessment | Market analysis, competitive landscape, SWOT | 2-3 weeks |
| Vision | Define 3-5 year direction | 1 week |
| Strategy | Choose strategic pillars and bets | 1-2 weeks |
| Planning | Set annual objectives and key results | 1-2 weeks |
| Alignment | Cascade to departments, resource allocation | 1-2 weeks |
| Communication | All-hands, documentation | 1 week |

### Competitive Analysis

| Dimension | Assessment |
|---|---|
| Market position | Leader, challenger, niche, follower |
| Differentiation | What makes us uniquely valuable? |
| Moat | Network effects, switching costs, data, brand |
| Threats | New entrants, substitutes, market shifts |
| Opportunities | Adjacent markets, underserved segments |

## 2. Fundraising

### Fundraising Stages

| Stage | Amount | Valuation | Purpose | Investors |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Pre-seed | $100K-$1M | $2-5M | Idea validation | Angels, pre-seed funds |
| Seed | $1-4M | $5-15M | Product-market fit | Seed funds, early VC |
| Series A | $5-20M | $20-80M | Scale go-to-market | Series A VCs |
| Series B | $20-60M | $80-300M | Accelerate growth | Growth VCs |
| Series C+ | $50-200M+ | $300M-$1B+ | Market dominance | Late-stage, PE |

### Pitch Deck Structure

| Slide | Content | Time |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Title | Company name, tagline, logo | 10 sec |
| 2. Problem | Pain point, market need | 1 min |
| 3. Solution | Your product/approach | 1 min |
| 4. Demo/Product | How it works | 2 min |
| 5. Market | TAM/SAM/SOM | 1 min |
| 6. Business model | How you make money | 1 min |
| 7. Traction | Metrics, growth, customers | 1 min |
| 8. Team | Founders, key hires | 1 min |
| 9. Competition | Positioning, differentiation | 1 min |
| 10. Financials | Revenue, projections, use of funds | 1 min |
| 11. Ask | Amount, terms, next steps | 30 sec |

### Investor Meeting Preparation

| Element | Preparation |
|---|---|
| Research | Know the fund's thesis, portfolio, partners |
| Story | Compelling narrative, not just data |
| Metrics | Key metrics memorized, trends explained |
| Objections | Prepare for top 10 likely questions |
| Ask | Clear amount, use of funds, milestones |
| Follow-up | Data room ready, references available |

## 3. Board Management

### Board Meeting Structure

| Section | Duration | Content |
|---|---|---|
| Opening | 5 min | Agenda, key decisions needed |
| CEO update | 15 min | Strategy, highlights, challenges |
| Financial review | 15 min | P&L, cash, metrics vs plan |
| Deep dive | 30 min | One strategic topic in depth |
| Discussion | 20 min | Board input, debate |
| Decisions | 10 min | Formal votes, approvals |
| Closed session | 15 min | Board-only discussion |

### Board Deck Template

```
1. Executive Summary (1 slide)
   - Key metrics vs plan
   - Top 3 wins
   - Top 3 challenges
   - Key decisions needed

2. Financial Performance (2-3 slides)
   - Revenue, ARR, growth rate
   - Burn rate, runway
   - Key metrics vs plan

3. Product & Engineering (1-2 slides)
   - Roadmap progress
   - Key launches
   - Technical milestones

4. Go-to-Market (1-2 slides)
   - Pipeline, conversion, churn
   - Key customer wins/losses
   - Market dynamics

5. Team & Organization (1 slide)
   - Headcount, hiring progress
   - Key hires, departures
   - Culture/engagement

6. Strategic Discussion (2-3 slides)
   - Deep dive topic
   - Options and tradeoffs
   - Recommendation

7. Appendix
   - Detailed financials
   - Metrics dashboard
   - Cap table (if relevant)
```

## 4. Company Building

### Stage-Appropriate Focus

| Stage | Employees | CEO Focus | Key Challenge |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pre-PMF | 1-10 | Product, customers, fundraising | Finding product-market fit |
| Early growth | 10-50 | Hiring, culture, go-to-market | Building the team |
| Scaling | 50-200 | Org design, processes, leadership | Managing complexity |
| Growth | 200-1000 | Strategy, culture, board | Maintaining velocity |
| Scale | 1000+ | Vision, M&A, market position | Avoiding bureaucracy |

### CEO Time Allocation

| Activity | Pre-PMF | Growth | Scale |
|---|---|---|---|
| Product/Customers | 40% | 20% | 10% |
| Hiring/People | 20% | 30% | 25% |
| Strategy | 10% | 15% | 25% |
| Fundraising/Board | 15% | 15% | 15% |
| External (PR, partnerships) | 5% | 10% | 15% |
| Operations | 10% | 10% | 10% |

## 5. Decision Frameworks

### Decision Quality Framework

| Factor | Question |
|---|---|
| Reversibility | Is this a one-way or two-way door? |
| Impact | How many people/dollars affected? |
| Urgency | What's the cost of delay? |
| Information | Do we have enough to decide? |
| Alignment | Who needs to agree? |

### RAPID Decision Framework

| Role | Description |
|---|---|
| Recommend | Proposes the decision, gathers input |
| Agree | Must agree (has veto power) |
| Perform | Executes the decision |
| Input | Consulted, provides perspective |
| Decide | Makes the final call |

### Type 1 vs Type 2 Decisions (Bezos)

| Type 1 | Type 2 |
|---|---|
| Irreversible | Reversible |
| High consequence | Low consequence |
| Require careful analysis | Can be made quickly |
| CEO/executive decision | Delegate to team |
| Example: M&A, pivots | Example: Feature prioritization |
