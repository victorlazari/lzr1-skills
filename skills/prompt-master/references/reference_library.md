# Curated Reference Library: AI Books (2025–2026)

This reference library compiles the top 20 books on RAG, prompt engineering, and LLM engineering published or updated in 2025 and 2026. Use this bibliography to find structured learning paths and conceptual patterns.

---

## Category A: AI Engineering & LLMOps

1. **AI Engineering: Building Applications with Foundation Models** (2025)  
   *Author:* Chip Huyen | *Publisher:* O'Reilly Media  
   *Key Topics:* Production stack design, quantitative RAG evaluation, multi-agent architectures, cost/latency/quality tradeoffs.
2. **LLM Engineer's Handbook** (2024/2025)  
   *Authors:* Paul Iusztin & Maxime Labonne | *Publisher:* Packt Publishing  
   *Key Topics:* End-to-end LLMOps pipelines, advanced RAG at scale, supervised fine-tuning, observability, and cost optimization.
3. **Building Reliable AI Systems** (2025)  
   *Author:* Rush Shahani | *Publisher:* Manning Publications  
   *Key Topics:* Hallucination mitigation, cost-performance tuning, bias monitoring, and production-grade guardrails.
4. **Building LLMs for Production** (2025)  
   *Authors:* Louis-François Bouchard & Louie Peters | *Publisher:* Independent  
   *Key Topics:* Supervised fine-tuning (SFT) recipes, scalable inference serving, LLM caching, and local serving infrastructure.
5. **Build a Large Language Model (from Scratch)** (2025)  
   *Author:* Sebastian Raschka | *Publisher:* Manning Publications  
   *Key Topics:* Implementing attention mechanisms, tokenization, pre-training, and SFT/RLHF alignment in PyTorch.

## Category B: Prompt Engineering & Cognitive Design

6. **AI Prompt Engineering Absolute Beginner's Guide** (2025)  
   *Author:* Michael Miller | *Publisher:* Pearson  
   *Key Topics:* Foundational prompt anatomy, few-shot techniques, role-based prompts, and structured user habits.
7. **Prompt Engineering for Generative AI** (2024/2025)  
   *Authors:* James Phoenix & Mike Taylor | *Publisher:* O'Reilly Media  
   *Key Topics:* Prompting as a repeatable operating system, programmatic evaluation, multimodal prompting, and ReAct planning loops.
8. **Prompt Engineering for LLMs** (2024/2025)  
   *Authors:* John Berryman & Albert Ziegler | *Publisher:* O'Reilly Media  
   *Key Topics:* In-context learning, CoT, simple RAG, and designing prompts directly inside application code.
9. **The Art of Prompt Engineering with ChatGPT (2026 Update)** (2026)  
   *Author:* Nathan Hunter | *Publisher:* Independent  
   *Key Topics:* Practical business use cases, custom GPT configurations, role-playing, and hands-on prompting exercises.
10. **Prompt Design Patterns** (2024/2025)  
    *Author:* Yi Zhou | *Publisher:* Independent  
    *Key Topics:* Structural prompt templates, modular prompting, meta-prompts, and programmatic generation pipelines.

## Category C: Advanced RAG & Agentic Systems

11. **Unlocking Data with Generative AI and RAG (2nd Edition)** (2025/2026)  
    *Author:* Keith Bourne | *Publisher:* Packt Publishing  
    *Key Topics:* Transitioning from classical to production RAG, GraphRAG, vector database management, and quantitative evaluation.
12. **A Simple Guide to Retrieval Augmented Generation** (2025)  
    *Author:* Abhinav Kimothi | *Publisher:* Manning Publications  
    *Key Topics:* Plain-English introduction, vector stores, chunking strategies, LlamaIndex, and practical Python pipelines.
13. **Building Agentic AI Systems** (2025)  
    *Authors:* Anjanava Biswas & Wrick Talukdar | *Publisher:* Packt Publishing  
    *Key Topics:* Autonomous multi-agent orchestration, tool-calling, memory management, and LangGraph/LangChain workflows.
14. **Hands-On Large Language Models** (2024/2025)  
    *Authors:* Jay Alammar & Maarten Grootendorst | *Publisher:* O'Reilly Media  
    *Key Topics:* Visual intuition of transformers, embeddings, semantic search, and fine-tuning.
15. **RAG-Driven Generative AI** (2025)  
    *Author:* Denis Rothman | *Publisher:* Packt Publishing  
    *Key Topics:* Building custom RAG pipelines, vector stores, chunking, indexing, and ranking with LlamaIndex.
16. **AI Agents in Action** (2025)  
    *Author:* Micheal Lanham | *Publisher:* Manning Publications  
    *Key Topics:* Designing autonomous agents, tool use, environment interaction, and task loops.
17. **Generative AI Design Patterns** (2024/2025)  
    *Authors:* Valliappa Lakshmanan & Hannes Hapke | *Publisher:* O'Reilly Media  
    *Key Topics:* Architectural patterns for RAG, prompt templates, agentic loops, and model routing.
18. **LLM Design Patterns** (2024/2025)  
    *Author:* Ken Huang | *Publisher:* Packt Publishing  
    *Key Topics:* System-level design patterns, security (prompt injection mitigation), and multi-modal handling.
19. **Mastering RAG for AI Agents** (2025)  
    *Authors:* David Spuler & Michael Sharpe | *Publisher:* Aussie AI  
    *Key Topics:* Connecting RAG to agentic workflows, GraphRAG, and memory-inspired knowledge retrieval.
20. **Retrieval Augmented Generation: The Seminal Papers** (2026)  
    *Author:* Manning Editors | *Publisher:* Manning Publications  
    *Key Topics:* Curated collection and deep technical commentary on foundational RAG papers.
