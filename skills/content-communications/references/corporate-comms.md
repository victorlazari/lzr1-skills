## 1. Internal Communications

### Communication Channels

| Channel | Best For | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| All-hands meeting | Company updates, strategy | Monthly/Quarterly |
| Email (CEO) | Important announcements | As needed |
| Slack/Teams | Daily updates, discussions | Continuous |
| Newsletter (internal) | Weekly highlights, wins | Weekly |
| Wiki/Notion | Policies, documentation | Updated as needed |
| Town hall | Q&A, transparency | Quarterly |
| 1:1 cascade | Sensitive changes | As needed |

### Internal Announcement Framework

```
1. Context: Why are we communicating this?
2. What: What's changing/happening?
3. Why: Why is this important?
4. Impact: How does this affect people?
5. Timeline: When does this happen?
6. Action: What do people need to do?
7. Support: Where to get help/ask questions?
```

### Change Communication Plan

| Phase | Message | Channel | Timing |
|---|---|---|---|
| Awareness | "Change is coming" | All-hands, email | 4-6 weeks before |
| Understanding | "Here's what and why" | Team meetings, FAQ | 2-4 weeks before |
| Preparation | "Here's how to prepare" | Training, guides | 1-2 weeks before |
| Launch | "It's happening now" | Email, Slack | Day of |
| Reinforcement | "How it's going" | Updates, feedback | Ongoing after |

## 2. Public Relations

### Press Release Structure

```
FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE

[Headline: Newsworthy, clear, <10 words]
[Subheadline: Additional context]

[City, State] — [Date] — [First paragraph: Who, what, when, where, why]

[Second paragraph: Supporting details, context]

[Quote from executive: Vision, significance]

[Third paragraph: Additional details, features, availability]

[Quote from customer/partner: Validation]

[Boilerplate: Company description, ~50 words]

###

Media Contact:
[Name], [Title]
[Email] | [Phone]
```

### Media Relations

| Activity | Purpose | Cadence |
|---|---|---|
| Press releases | Major announcements | As needed |
| Media briefings | Deep-dive with journalists | Quarterly |
| Byline articles | Thought leadership | Monthly |
| Analyst briefings | Industry positioning | Quarterly |
| Award submissions | Recognition, credibility | Ongoing |
| Speaker placements | Conference visibility | Quarterly |

## 3. Crisis Communications

### Crisis Response Framework

| Phase | Action | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| Detect | Identify the crisis, assess severity | Immediate |
| Assemble | Activate crisis team | Within 30 minutes |
| Assess | Determine facts, impact, stakeholders | Within 1 hour |
| Respond | First public communication | Within 2-4 hours |
| Update | Regular updates as situation evolves | Every 2-4 hours |
| Resolve | Announce resolution | When resolved |
| Review | Post-mortem, lessons learned | Within 1 week |

### Crisis Communication Template

```
[What happened]
We became aware of [issue] at [time] on [date].

[Impact]
This affected [scope of impact].

[What we did]
Our team immediately [actions taken].

[Current status]
As of [time], [current state].

[What we're doing to prevent recurrence]
We are [preventive measures].

[Apology/Accountability]
We take this seriously and apologize for [impact].

[Next steps]
We will provide another update by [time].
```

## 4. Executive Communications

### Executive Thought Leadership

| Format | Platform | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn posts | LinkedIn | 2-3x/week |
| Blog posts | Company blog | 1-2x/month |
| Conference talks | Industry events | Quarterly |
| Podcast appearances | Industry podcasts | Monthly |
| Byline articles | Industry publications | Monthly |
| Twitter/X threads | Twitter | Weekly |

### Ghostwriting Process

| Step | Activity |
|---|---|
| 1 | Brief: Topic, angle, audience, key points |
| 2 | Interview: 15-30 min conversation with exec |
| 3 | Outline: Structure and key messages |
| 4 | Draft: First draft in exec's voice |
| 5 | Review: Exec reviews, provides feedback |
| 6 | Finalize: Incorporate feedback, polish |
| 7 | Publish: Post on appropriate platform |
