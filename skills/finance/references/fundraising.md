## 1. Fundraising Process

### Fundraising Timeline

| Phase | Duration | Activities |
|---|---|---|
| Preparation | 4-8 weeks | Materials, data room, target list |
| Outreach | 2-4 weeks | Warm intros, initial meetings |
| Diligence | 4-8 weeks | Deep dives, reference checks |
| Term sheet | 1-2 weeks | Negotiation, selection |
| Legal/Close | 4-6 weeks | Documentation, signing, funding |

### Funding Stages

| Stage | Typical Amount | Valuation Range | Key Metrics |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pre-seed | $500K-$2M | $5-15M | Team, idea, early traction |
| Seed | $2-5M | $10-30M | MVP, early customers, PMF signals |
| Series A | $10-25M | $40-100M | PMF, $1-3M ARR, growth rate |
| Series B | $25-75M | $100-400M | Scaling, $5-20M ARR, unit economics |
| Series C+ | $50-200M+ | $300M-$1B+ | Market leadership, path to IPO |

## 2. Valuation Methods

### Valuation Approaches

| Method | Description | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| Revenue multiple | Enterprise Value / ARR | Growth-stage SaaS |
| DCF | Discounted future cash flows | Profitable companies |
| Comparable companies | Multiples from public peers | Benchmarking |
| Precedent transactions | Multiples from M&A deals | M&A context |
| Last round + growth | Prior valuation × growth factor | Quick estimate |
| Scorecard method | Weighted factor comparison | Early stage |

### SaaS Valuation Multiples (2024-2025)

| Growth Rate | Median EV/Revenue | Top Quartile |
|---|---|---|
| <20% | 4-6x | 8-10x |
| 20-40% | 6-10x | 12-15x |
| 40-60% | 10-15x | 15-20x |
| 60-100% | 15-25x | 25-40x |
| 100%+ | 25-50x | 50x+ |

### Factors Affecting Multiples

| Factor | Higher Multiple | Lower Multiple |
|---|---|---|
| Growth rate | >50% YoY | <20% YoY |
| Net retention | >130% | <100% |
| Gross margin | >80% | <60% |
| Market size | Large TAM ($10B+) | Small niche |
| Profitability | Rule of 40+ | High burn |
| Competitive position | Market leader | Crowded market |

## 4. Term Sheets

### Key Term Sheet Terms

| Term | Description | Founder Impact |
|---|---|---|
| Valuation (pre-money) | Company value before investment | Dilution |
| Option pool | Reserved for future employees | Effective dilution |
| Liquidation preference | Payout order in exit | Downside protection for investors |
| Participation | Double-dip on liquidation | Reduces founder proceeds |
| Anti-dilution | Price protection for down rounds | Weighted average vs full ratchet |
| Board seats | Governance control | Decision-making power |
| Protective provisions | Investor veto rights | Operational constraints |
| Pro-rata rights | Right to invest in future rounds | Investor maintains ownership |
| Drag-along | Force sale if majority agrees | Exit flexibility |
| Information rights | Financial reporting requirements | Administrative burden |

### Dilution Example

```
Pre-money valuation: $20M
Investment: $5M
Post-money valuation: $25M
Investor ownership: $5M / $25M = 20%

If 10% option pool created pre-money:
  Effective pre-money (founders): $20M - ($25M × 10%) = $17.5M
  Founder dilution is greater than headline suggests
```

## 5. Cap Table Management

### Cap Table Structure

| Stakeholder | Shares | % Ownership | Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| Founder 1 | 4,000,000 | 40% | Common |
| Founder 2 | 3,000,000 | 30% | Common |
| Seed investors | 1,500,000 | 15% | Preferred (Series Seed) |
| Option pool (allocated) | 800,000 | 8% | Options |
| Option pool (unallocated) | 700,000 | 7% | Reserved |
| **Total** | **10,000,000** | **100%** | |

### 409A Valuation

```
Required for:
- Setting option exercise prices
- Compliance with IRC Section 409A
- Typically refreshed every 12 months or after material events

Methods:
- Market approach (comparable companies)
- Income approach (DCF)
- Asset approach (early stage)

Common discount from preferred price: 50-80% (early stage)
```
