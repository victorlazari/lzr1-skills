## 1. Recruiting Strategy

### Hiring Plan Components

| Component | Description | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| Headcount plan | Roles needed by quarter | Finance + hiring managers |
| Priority ranking | Order of hiring importance | Leadership |
| Job architecture | Levels, titles, requirements | HR + hiring managers |
| Sourcing strategy | Where to find candidates | Recruiting |
| Interview process | Steps, interviewers, criteria | Recruiting + hiring managers |
| Employer brand | How we attract talent | Marketing + HR |
| Budget | Agency, tools, events, referrals | Finance + HR |

### Hiring Timeline (Tech Roles)

| Stage | Duration | Activities |
|---|---|---|
| Req approval | 1-2 weeks | Business case, headcount approval |
| Sourcing | 2-4 weeks | Job posting, outreach, referrals |
| Screening | 1-2 weeks | Resume review, recruiter screens |
| Interviews | 2-3 weeks | Technical, behavioral, culture |
| Decision | 1 week | Debrief, calibration, decision |
| Offer | 1-2 weeks | Approval, negotiation, acceptance |
| **Total** | **8-14 weeks** | |

## 2. Sourcing

### Sourcing Channels

| Channel | Best For | Cost | Quality |
|---|---|---|---|
| Employee referrals | All roles | Medium (bonus) | Highest |
| LinkedIn Recruiter | Passive candidates | High | High |
| Job boards (LinkedIn, Indeed) | Active candidates | Medium | Medium |
| GitHub/Stack Overflow | Engineers | Low-Medium | High |
| University recruiting | Entry-level | Medium | Variable |
| Agencies/Headhunters | Executive, niche | Very High (20-30%) | High |
| Events/Meetups | Community building | Low | Medium |
| Inbound (careers page) | Brand-attracted | Low | Variable |

### Job Description Best Practices

| Section | Content | Tips |
|---|---|---|
| Title | Clear, standard title | Avoid creative titles that confuse |
| Hook | Why this role matters | 2-3 sentences, compelling |
| Responsibilities | What you'll do (5-7 bullets) | Action verbs, specific |
| Requirements | Must-have qualifications | Keep to true requirements |
| Nice-to-haves | Preferred but not required | Clearly separate from requirements |
| Benefits | Compensation, perks, culture | Be specific, include ranges |
| About us | Company mission, stage, culture | Authentic, concise |

## 3. Interview Process

### Structured Interview Design

| Round | Purpose | Duration | Interviewer |
|---|---|---|---|
| Recruiter screen | Fit, motivation, logistics | 30 min | Recruiter |
| Hiring manager screen | Role fit, experience depth | 45 min | Hiring manager |
| Technical assessment | Skills validation | 60-90 min | Technical team |
| Cross-functional | Collaboration, culture | 45 min | Adjacent team |
| Leadership/Values | Culture add, growth potential | 30-45 min | Senior leader |

### Scorecard Template

```
Candidate: [Name]
Role: [Position]
Interviewer: [Name]
Date: [Date]

Competencies (1-5 scale):
1. [Technical skill 1]: ___
2. [Technical skill 2]: ___
3. [Collaboration]: ___
4. [Communication]: ___
5. [Problem-solving]: ___
6. [Culture alignment]: ___

Overall recommendation: Strong Yes / Yes / No / Strong No

Key observations:
- Strengths: [specific examples]
- Concerns: [specific examples]
- Notes: [additional context]
```

### Interview Best Practices

| Practice | Description |
|---|---|
| Structured questions | Same questions for all candidates in same role |
| Behavioral format | "Tell me about a time when..." (past behavior) |
| Scoring rubric | Pre-defined criteria for each score level |
| Independent scoring | Score before debrief to avoid groupthink |
| Diverse panel | Multiple perspectives, reduce bias |
| Timely feedback | Score within 24 hours of interview |

## 4. Offer and Closing

### Offer Components

| Component | Description | Negotiable |
|---|---|---|
| Base salary | Fixed annual compensation | Yes (within band) |
| Equity/Options | Stock options or RSUs | Yes |
| Sign-on bonus | One-time joining bonus | Yes |
| Target bonus | Variable compensation | Sometimes |
| Start date | When they begin | Yes |
| Title/Level | Role and seniority | Sometimes |
| Benefits | Health, 401k, perks | Usually fixed |
| Remote/Location | Work arrangement | Sometimes |

### Closing Strategies

| Situation | Strategy |
|---|---|
| Competing offer | Speed up process, highlight differentiators |
| Compensation gap | Equity, sign-on bonus, growth trajectory |
| Risk-averse | Stability signals, team introductions |
| Counteroffer risk | Understand motivations beyond money |
| Relocation hesitancy | Relocation package, remote flexibility |
| Multiple stakeholders | Address each decision-maker's concerns |

## 5. Recruiting Metrics

### Key Metrics

| Metric | Formula | Benchmark |
|---|---|---|
| Time to fill | Days from req open to offer accepted | 30-60 days (tech) |
| Time to hire | Days from first contact to offer accepted | 20-40 days |
| Offer acceptance rate | Offers accepted / Offers extended | >85% |
| Quality of hire | Performance rating at 6/12 months | >80% meeting expectations |
| Source effectiveness | Hires per source / Cost per source | Varies by channel |
| Pipeline conversion | Candidates advancing per stage | Funnel analysis |
| Diversity | % underrepresented at each stage | Improving trend |
| Cost per hire | Total recruiting cost / Hires | $4-8K (in-house) |
| Recruiter productivity | Hires per recruiter per month | 3-5 (tech) |
