## 1. Compensation Philosophy

### Philosophy Components

| Component | Options | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Market position | 25th, 50th, 75th, 90th percentile | Where you target vs market |
| Pay mix | Base-heavy vs equity-heavy | Fixed vs variable balance |
| Geographic policy | Location-based vs location-agnostic | Pay by location or not |
| Transparency | Closed, partial, full | How much is shared |
| Equity approach | Broad-based vs selective | Who gets equity |

### Market Positioning Strategy

| Percentile | Strategy | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| 50th (median) | Market competitive | Stable companies, strong brand |
| 60-75th | Above market | Competitive hiring, retention focus |
| 75-90th | Premium | Top talent, high-growth, well-funded |
| 90th+ | Best-in-class | FAANG-level, critical roles |

## 2. Salary Bands

### Band Structure

```
Level: Senior Software Engineer (L3)
Band: $160,000 - $200,000 - $240,000
       (min)      (mid)      (max)

Position in band:
- New to role: 80-90% of midpoint (min to mid)
- Fully performing: 90-110% of midpoint (around mid)
- Expert/ready for promotion: 100-120% of midpoint (mid to max)
```

### Band Design Principles

| Principle | Description |
|---|---|
| Market-anchored | Midpoints based on market data |
| Consistent spread | Typically 20-25% above/below midpoint |
| Level differentiation | 15-25% increase between levels |
| Overlap allowed | Adjacent bands may overlap slightly |
| Regular updates | Review annually against market |
| Geographic adjustment | Apply location factors if applicable |

### Geographic Pay Approach

| Approach | Description | Pros | Cons |
|---|---|---|---|
| Location-based | Pay by local market | Cost-effective | Complexity, perceived unfairness |
| Location-agnostic | Same pay everywhere | Simple, fair | Expensive in low-cost areas |
| Zone-based | 3-4 tiers by cost of living | Balanced | Still some complexity |
| HQ-anchored | % of HQ rate by location | Clear anchor | Arbitrary for remote |

## 3. Equity Compensation

### Equity Types

| Type | Description | Tax Treatment | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| ISO (Incentive Stock Options) | Right to buy at strike price | Favorable (AMT risk) | Early-stage employees |
| NSO (Non-Qualified Options) | Right to buy at strike price | Ordinary income at exercise | Contractors, late grants |
| RSU (Restricted Stock Units) | Shares granted, vest over time | Ordinary income at vest | Public/late-stage companies |
| Restricted Stock | Actual shares with restrictions | 83(b) election available | Very early employees |

### Vesting Schedules

| Schedule | Description | Common For |
|---|---|---|
| 4-year, 1-year cliff | 25% after year 1, monthly thereafter | Standard startup |
| 4-year, monthly | Equal monthly vesting from day 1 | Employee-friendly |
| 3-year, annual | 33% per year | Some public companies |
| 4-year, back-loaded | More vests in later years | Amazon-style retention |
| Performance-based | Vests on achieving milestones | Executive grants |

### Equity Grant Guidelines (Startup)

| Level | Equity Range (% of company) | Context |
|---|---|---|
| Early employees (#1-10) | 0.5-2.0% | High risk, high reward |
| Early employees (#11-30) | 0.1-0.5% | Still early, meaningful |
| Senior IC (post-Series A) | 0.05-0.2% | Market-competitive |
| Manager (post-Series A) | 0.1-0.3% | Leadership premium |
| Director (post-Series B) | 0.05-0.15% | Smaller pool, larger company |
| VP (post-Series B) | 0.1-0.5% | Executive-level |

## 4. Benefits Design

### Core Benefits Package

| Benefit | Standard | Premium |
|---|---|---|
| Health insurance | 80% employer-paid | 100% employee + family |
| Dental/Vision | Basic coverage | Full coverage |
| 401(k)/Retirement | Match up to 3-4% | Match up to 6%+ |
| PTO | 15-20 days | Unlimited (with minimum) |
| Parental leave | 12 weeks | 16-20 weeks |
| Life insurance | 1x salary | 2x salary |
| Disability | Short + long-term | Enhanced coverage |
| Mental health | EAP | Therapy stipend |
| Learning | $1-2K/year | $5K+/year |
| Remote stipend | $500-1K setup | $200/month ongoing |

|---|---|

| Financial | Financial planning, student loan assistance, emergency fund |
| Family | Fertility benefits, childcare, elder care |
| Development | Conference budget, book budget, course reimbursement |

## 5. Compensation Reviews

### Annual Compensation Review Process

| Phase | Timeline | Activities |
|---|---|---|
| Market data refresh | Month 1 | Update salary bands with new market data |
| Budget allocation | Month 2 | Determine merit, promotion, equity budgets |
| Manager recommendations | Month 3 | Managers propose adjustments |
| Calibration | Month 3-4 | Leadership reviews for consistency |
| Approval | Month 4 | Final approval from leadership |
| Communication | Month 4-5 | Managers communicate to employees |
| Implementation | Month 5 | Payroll updates, equity grants |

### Merit Increase Guidelines

| Performance Rating | Typical Merit Increase | Equity Refresh |
|---|---|---|
| Exceptional | 8-12% | 1.5-2x standard grant |
| Exceeds | 5-8% | 1.2-1.5x standard grant |
| Meets | 3-5% | Standard grant |
| Developing | 0-2% | Reduced or none |
| Below | 0% | None |

### Pay Equity Analysis

| Analysis | Purpose | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Gender pay gap | Equal pay for equal work | Annual |
| Ethnicity pay gap | Equitable across demographics | Annual |
| Compa-ratio distribution | Position in band by group | Annual |
| New hire vs tenure | Fair treatment of existing employees | Semi-annual |
| Promotion equity | Equal access to advancement | Annual |
