## 1. Vision and Strategy

### Strategy Stack

| Level | Timeframe | Owner | Output |
|---|---|---|---|
| Company mission | Permanent | CEO/Founders | Mission statement |
| Company strategy | 3-5 years | Leadership | Strategic plan |
| Product vision | 2-3 years | CPO/VP Product | Vision statement |
| Product strategy | 1-2 years | Product leaders | Strategy document |
| Product roadmap | 3-12 months | Product managers | Roadmap |
| Sprint goals | 1-4 weeks | Product team | Sprint backlog |

### Product Vision Template

```
FOR [target customer]
WHO [statement of need or opportunity]
THE [product name] IS A [product category]
THAT [key benefit, reason to buy]
UNLIKE [competitive alternative]
OUR PRODUCT [primary differentiation]
```

### Strategy Components

| Component | Question | Output |
|---|---|---|
| Market | Where do we play? | Target segments, geographies |
| Value proposition | How do we win? | Unique value, differentiation |
| Business model | How do we capture value? | Revenue model, pricing |
| Competitive advantage | Why us? | Moats, unfair advantages |
| Growth strategy | How do we grow? | Channels, expansion paths |

## 2. Market Analysis

### Market Sizing

| Method | Description | Use Case |
|---|---|---|
| TAM | Total Addressable Market | Maximum opportunity |
| SAM | Serviceable Addressable Market | Realistic addressable |
| SOM | Serviceable Obtainable Market | Near-term target |
| Bottom-up | Customer count × ARPU | More accurate |
| Top-down | Industry size × capture % | Quick estimate |

### Competitive Analysis Framework

| Dimension | Questions | Sources |
|---|---|---|
| Product | Features, UX, technology | Product usage, reviews |
| Business model | Pricing, revenue model | Public data, pricing pages |
| Market position | Share, growth, perception | Analyst reports, press |
| Strengths | What they do well | Customer reviews, awards |
| Weaknesses | Where they fall short | Complaints, churn reasons |
| Strategy | Where they're heading | Job postings, releases, patents |

### Porter's Five Forces

| Force | Description | Impact on Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| Rivalry | Competition intensity | Differentiation need |
| New entrants | Barriers to entry | Moat building |
| Substitutes | Alternative solutions | Value proposition clarity |
| Buyer power | Customer negotiation leverage | Switching cost strategy |
| Supplier power | Dependency on suppliers | Diversification |

## 3. Positioning

### Positioning Statement

```
For [target segment] who [need/pain point],
[Product] is a [category] that [key benefit].
Unlike [alternatives], we [differentiator].
```

### Category Design

| Strategy | Description | When to Use |
|---|---|---|
| Category leader | Dominate existing category | Strong market position |
| Category challenger | Reframe existing category | Differentiated approach |
| Category creator | Define new category | Novel solution, no clear fit |
| Niche specialist | Own a specific segment | Focused, underserved market |

## 4. Roadmapping

### Roadmap Types

| Type | Audience | Content | Timeframe |
|---|---|---|---|
| Strategic | Executives, board | Themes, outcomes | 12-18 months |
| Release | Stakeholders, sales | Features, dates | 3-6 months |
| Discovery | Product team | Experiments, hypotheses | 1-3 months |
| Now/Next/Later | Everyone | Priorities without dates | Rolling |

### Roadmap Best Practices

| Practice | Description |
|---|---|
| Outcome-oriented | Frame as problems to solve, not features to build |
| Flexible | Avoid committing to specific dates far out |
| Evidence-based | Link items to customer evidence and data |
| Balanced | Mix quick wins, strategic bets, and tech debt |
| Communicated | Regular updates, transparent changes |
| Reviewed | Quarterly review and adjustment |

## 5. Prioritization Frameworks

### Framework Comparison

| Framework | Factors | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| RICE | Reach, Impact, Confidence, Effort | Quantitative scoring |
| ICE | Impact, Confidence, Ease | Quick prioritization |
| MoSCoW | Must, Should, Could, Won't | Release scoping |
| Kano | Delight, Performance, Basic | Feature categorization |
| Value vs Effort | Business value, implementation cost | Visual prioritization |
| Opportunity Score | Importance, Satisfaction | Jobs-to-be-done |
| Cost of Delay | Value over time, urgency | Time-sensitive decisions |

### RICE Scoring

```
RICE Score = (Reach × Impact × Confidence) / Effort

Reach: Users affected per quarter (number)
Impact: Effect on individual user (0.25, 0.5, 1, 2, 3)
Confidence: Certainty of estimates (100%, 80%, 50%)
Effort: Person-months of work (number)
```

### Prioritization Decision Matrix

| Quadrant | Value | Effort | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Quick wins | High | Low | Do first |
| Big bets | High | High | Plan carefully |
| Fill-ins | Low | Low | Do if time allows |
| Money pits | Low | High | Don't do |
